Purpose
The Methods Watchlist is maintained by the Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) team to track adversary techniques (TTPs) that are actively used in real-world attacks against the financial sector. Its purpose is to provide visibility into how attacks are executed, enabling improved campaign tracking, threat analysis, and intelligence reporting. By focusing on commonly observed and high-risk techniques, the watchlist supports a better understanding of attacker behavior and helps inform defensive priorities.

Scope
The scope of this watchlist is limited to methods that align with the organization’s security posture, control environment, and risk profile within the financial sector. It focuses on techniques that are relevant to how the business operates, the technologies it uses, and the threats most likely to impact its assets and operations. Priority is given to methods observed in recent or ongoing activity targeting banking and financial institutions in countries or regions where the business has operational presence or strategic interest. The watchlist maintains a focus on simple, trackable technique-level entries compatible with Recorded Future capabilities and is curated based on validated intelligence from multiple trusted sources. It is manually maintained by the CTI team and updated on a bi-weekly to monthly cadence, with additional updates as needed for emerging threats.

Threat Actor Watchlist

Purpose
The Threat Actor Watchlist is maintained by the Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) team to monitor adversaries actively targeting the financial sector. Its purpose is to provide visibility into who is conducting attacks, support tracking of campaigns and targeting trends, and enhance intelligence-driven decision-making and reporting. By maintaining a focused list of relevant threat actors, the watchlist helps prioritize monitoring efforts and improves situational awareness of the evolving threat landscape.

Scope
The scope of this watchlist includes financially motivated cybercriminal groups, ransomware operators, and advanced persistent threat (APT) groups that have demonstrated activity against banking and financial institutions. Focus is placed on threat actors that pose risk to the organization based on its business operations, technology footprint, and threat exposure. Priority is given to actors targeting countries or regions where the business has operational presence or strategic interest within the financial sector. Threat actors are selected based on recent activity, demonstrated capability, and validation across multiple reputable intelligence sources. The list is manually maintained by the CTI team, reviewed on a bi-weekly to monthly cadence, and updated as needed to reflect changes in active threats and targeting patterns.
