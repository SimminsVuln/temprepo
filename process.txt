# 📄 Threat Actor Watchlist Process (Recorded Future)

## 🟢 1. Intelligence Gathering & Candidate Identification

* The **Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) team** continuously collects intelligence from multiple sources:

  * Feedly for aggregated threat reporting and vendor advisories
  * CrowdStrike intelligence reports and threat actor tracking
  * Anvilogic detection insights tied to attacker behaviors
  * FS-ISAC sector-specific threat alerts
  * Recorded Future trending threat actors, campaigns, and risk scores
* Additional inputs:

  * Campaign reports and malware analysis
  * Incident response findings and internal SIRs
  * Threat actor infrastructure or targeting trends
* Identify candidate threat actors that:

  * Are actively conducting **campaigns or attacks**
  * Target **banking / financial institutions**
  * Have activity tied to **US and/or India regions**

---

## 🟡 2. Threat Actor Validation & Relevance Assessment

* Confirm the threat actor is:

  * Recognized across **reputable intelligence sources**
  * Actively associated with **recent campaigns or operations**
* Ensure the actor meets **all required criteria**:

  * Demonstrated activity within the **last 6–12 months**
  * Associated with:

    * Malware
    * Exploitation activity
    * Credential theft / fraud / ransomware / intrusion activity
  * Directly relevant to:

    * **Financial sector targeting**
    * **US and/or India**
* Preference is given to actors that:

  * Appear in **multiple intelligence sources**
  * Show **consistent or repeated targeting** of financial organizations
  * Are linked to **high-impact activity**, such as:

    * Financial theft (fraud, SWIFT, carding)
    * Ransomware attacks
    * Account takeover / identity attacks

---

## 🔵 3. Threat Actor Selection Criteria

* A threat actor is approved for addition if it meets one or more of the following:

  * Observed in **multiple intelligence sources**
  * Linked to a **notable or trending campaign**
  * Associated with **active exploitation or intrusion activity**
  * Demonstrates **clear impact to financial sector operations**
* Avoid adding actors that are:

  * Poorly attributed or low-confidence
  * Not recently active
  * Not relevant to financial sector targeting

---

## 🔷 4. Add Threat Actor to Recorded Future Watchlist

* Navigate to **Threat Actors → Watchlists** in Recorded Future
* Open the **Threat Actor Watchlist**
* Add new entry:

  * **Threat Actor Name** (standardized naming, e.g., FIN7, APT28)
* Save and verify the actor is successfully added

---

## 🟣 5. Review & Maintenance (Bi-Weekly / Monthly)

* Conduct review on a **bi-weekly or monthly cadence**
* During review:

  * Reassess each actor using updated intelligence from:

    * Feedly
    * CrowdStrike
    * Anvilogic
    * FS-ISAC
    * Recorded Future
  * Evaluate:

    * Continued activity in campaigns
    * Ongoing relevance to financial sector (US/India)
* Actions:

  * **Add** newly identified threat actors
  * **Retain** actors still actively targeting relevant sectors
  * **Remove** actors no longer relevant (based on team review)

---

## 🔴 6. Trending / Out-of-Cycle Updates

* If a **high-impact or rapidly emerging threat actor** is identified:

  * Add immediately outside of scheduled review cycle
* Triggers include:

  * Active campaigns targeting financial institutions
  * Ransomware groups impacting banking sector
  * Alerts from FS-ISAC or major intelligence providers
* Ensure rapid validation before addition

---

## ⚫ 7. Ownership & Governance

* The **Cyber Threat Intelligence team** is responsible for:

  * Intelligence collection and analysis
  * Threat actor validation and selection
  * Watchlist updates and maintenance
* All actions are:

  * **Manually performed**
  * **Reviewed during scheduled cycles or as needed**

---

## ✅ Outcome

* Maintains a **focused, high-confidence Threat Actor Watchlist**
* Ensures alignment with:

  * Active threat landscape
  * Financial sector targeting (US/India)
* Enables:

  * Improved tracking of adversary activity
  * Better campaign awareness
  * Stronger intelligence-driven decision making

# 📄 Methods Watchlist Process (Recorded Future)

## 🟢 1. Intelligence Gathering & Candidate Identification

* The **Cyber Threat Intelligence (CTI) team** continuously collects intelligence from multiple sources:

  * Feedly for aggregated threat reporting and vendor advisories
  * CrowdStrike intelligence reports and threat research
  * Anvilogic detection insights and attack patterns
  * FS-ISAC sector-specific threat intelligence
  * Recorded Future trending vulnerabilities, malware, and campaigns
* Additional inputs:

  * Active campaign reporting
  * Malware analysis and write-ups
  * Exploited vulnerabilities and intrusion techniques
* Identify candidate methods (TTPs) that:

  * Appear across **multiple intelligence sources** OR
  * Are tied to **high-confidence reporting from a trusted source**
  * Are actively used in **real-world campaigns**

---

## 🟡 2. Method Validation & Relevance Assessment

* Validate candidate methods against MITRE ATT&CK
* Confirm the method meets **all required criteria**:

  * Observed in a **recent or active campaign**
  * Associated with:

    * Malware
    * Exploitation activity
    * Threat actor tradecraft
  * Directly relevant to:

    * **Banking / financial sector targets**
    * **US and/or India regions**
* Preference is given to methods that:

  * Are used by **multiple threat actors or campaigns**
  * Represent **high-risk behaviors**, such as:

    * Credential access
    * Phishing / initial access
    * Lateral movement
    * Data exfiltration
* Keep scope at a **simple TTP level (Txxxx)** aligned with Recorded Future watchlist capabilities

---

## 🔵 3. Method Selection Criteria

* A method is approved for addition if it meets one or more of the following:

  * Observed in **multiple intelligence sources**
  * Linked to a **notable or trending campaign**
  * Associated with **active exploitation in the wild**
  * Demonstrates **clear impact to financial sector operations**
* Avoid adding methods that are:

  * Too generic without clear campaign usage
  * Not recently observed or relevant
  * Not tied to financial sector targeting

---

## 🔷 4. Add Method to Recorded Future Watchlist

* Navigate to **Methods → Watchlists** in Recorded Future
* Open the **Methods Watchlist**
* Add new entry:

  * **Technique ID (Txxxx)**
  * **Method Name (MITRE-aligned)**
* Save and verify the method is successfully added

---

## 🟣 5. Review & Maintenance (Bi-Weekly / Monthly)

* Conduct formal review on a **bi-weekly or monthly cadence**
* During review:

  * Reassess each method using updated intelligence from:

    * Feedly
    * CrowdStrike
    * Anvilogic
    * FS-ISAC
    * Recorded Future
  * Evaluate:

    * Continued use in active campaigns
    * Ongoing relevance to financial sector (US/India)
* Actions:

  * **Add** newly identified methods
  * **Retain** methods still actively observed
  * **Remove** methods no longer relevant (based on team consensus)

---

## 🔴 6. Trending / Out-of-Cycle Updates

* If intelligence sources indicate a **high-impact or rapidly emerging threat**:

  * Add the method immediately (outside normal cadence)
* Triggers include:

  * Widespread exploitation
  * High-profile campaign affecting financial institutions
  * Alerts from FS-ISAC or major vendors
* Ensure rapid validation before addition

---

## ⚫ 7. Ownership & Governance

* The **Cyber Threat Intelligence team** is responsible for:

  * Intelligence collection and analysis
  * Method validation and selection
  * Watchlist updates and maintenance
* All actions are:

  * **Manually performed**
  * **Reviewed during scheduled cycles or as needed**

---

## ✅ Outcome

* Maintains a **high-confidence, intelligence-driven Methods Watchlist**
* Ensures alignment with:

  * Real-world threat activity
  * Financial sector targeting (US/India)
* Enables:

  * Improved threat tracking
  * Better campaign awareness
  * More informed downstream analysis and reporting
